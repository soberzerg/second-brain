## 2. Business Strategist

```
Act as a business strategist

Prepare a business plan for the following business niche idea:

[copy/paste niche idea details]

Prepare a plan that follows these guidelines:

The business follows the BATON system of Business (niche), Audience, Tribe, Offer, Network.

Business = defining the value generated, the customer definition, the product-market fit, defining the customer‚Äôs primary problem.

Audience = getting the value of the business out to the ideal customers, content marketing based around the customer‚Äôs primary problem, wide attention grabbing content on social media (preference Twitter)

Tribe = capturing customer details (email) via lead magnet and email newsletter, building rapport and trust

Offer = after audience growth and tribe trust build the offer is made, sales funnel from low cost tripwire digital product ($) to core digital product ($$) to service offering ($$$) all addressing the same customer problem but moving from DIY to done for you.

Network = scaling the business by adding more customers and / or selling more products and services to the same customer pool.
```

This prompt will take the chosen business niche and formulate a business plan for you.

It‚Äôs based of the BATON framework that I teach.

**Business** - what is the value your business creates?

**Audience**- how will you let the world know about this value?

**Tribe** - how will people raise their hand and tell you they want to know more?

**Offer** - how will you package up and sell the value?

**Network** - how will you scale up?

## 3. Competitive Analysis

Here‚Äôs the prompt:

```
Act as a business competitor analyst

First, assess the above business and provide a list of competitors in the niche

Second, for each competitors scan their website, information to complete the following table: name, website, unique positioning, strengths, weaknesses

Third, based on the table provide 3 potential positioning strategy for my business to carve a space in the market
```

As an entrepreneur you need to learn to love competition.

Sounds weird right?

But competition in a market means that a market exists!

If there‚Äôs no competition then, chances are, there‚Äôs no market. People have tried and failed to build sustainable businesses.

If there are existing companies (competitors) in a market then customers, revenue and profits exist. Otherwise the competitors would have ceased to exist!

Sounds paradoxical right? But grasping this separates the amateurs from the pros.

Competition risk is better than market risk!

Competitors we can outmanoeuvre. A non-existent market we cannot.

## 7. Lead Magnet

Here‚Äôs the prompt:

```
Act as a social media manager for Twitter

Use the provided content plan to produce tweets for the content pillar [number]

Provide 10 short, 5 medium posts in full. And 5 long post ideas.
Do not use hashtags or emojis.

Short tweets should be 1-2 sentences, impactful, thought provoking, controversial, challenging.

Medium tweets should use the full 180 characters and show specific domain expertise, avoiding platitudes, ask a questions to begin discussion.

Long tweets should be a thread format (multiple individual tweets) chained together. Provide ideas.
```

```
Act as a social media manager for Instagram

Use the provided content plan to produce reels for the content pillar [number]

Provide 10 reel ideas.
```

This prompt takes your content marketing plan from the last lesson and converts it into actual content.

Remember to add the content pillar number in line 2.

If you try to do _all_ the pillars you‚Äôll get a output but the quality will tend to be lower as ChatGPT will be trying to fit too much into the output.

You can rerun this prompt as often as required to generate more content. This is also a great chance to work on refining a prompt - basically as you use a prompt multiple times it can be helpful to adjust it so that your outcomes are better.

For example perhaps you notice the prompt keeps giving you overly formal outputs. You could adjust the prompt by adding a line like ‚ÄúMake it humorous‚Äù to test if the results are better.

If you like them more you can then leave that extra line in the prompt when you use it in the future.

This process of adjusting and improving prompts if called prompt engineering. This reusable Twitter content prompt is a great chance to practice!
